Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

To tell if your helmet needs replacing, check if it's over 3-5 years old, has visible cracks or dents, worn padding, damaged straps, or if it's been in a crash. If the helmet feels loose or outdated in safety standards, it's time for a new one.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding a bike that’s been in storage, check the tires for cracks or flat spots, ensure the brakes work properly, inspect the chain for rust, and test the gears. Also, make sure the frame is free from damage and the wheels are aligned.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding your bike, always wear a helmet, follow traffic signals, ride with the flow of traffic, use hand signals when turning, stay in bike lanes when available, and be aware of your surroundings. Make sure your bike has lights and reflectors if riding at night, and always yield to pedestrians.
